Text 2 Chart

Convert your text data into visual charts effortlessly with our Text to Chart feature.


Text-to-image models learn to generate images that match a user’s prompt from details in their training datasets’ images and captions. But the quality of detail and accuracy in these pairings can vary widely for each image and caption.

Text 2 Chart works within a specific process with datasets tailored to it. In the first stage, it recognizes x-axis and y-axis entities from the input text. In the second stage, it maps x-axis entities with its corresponding y-axis entities and in the third stage, it predicts the best suited chart type for the particular text input. There are many related works however they are not efficient and only focus on a single component. Text 2 Chart works by identifying the data and summarising it

By automating the conversion process, Text 2 Chart streamlines data visualization workflows, saving time and reducing the risk of errors associated with manual entry. Moreover, it empowers users with limited data analysis expertise to generate professional-quality visualizations without the need for specialized skills or software.



Stage 1: x-Axis and y-Axis Label Entity Recognition

Neural Networks were built which were trained and fed with representations of words in a way that a computer can understand. Seperate models are built to identify the X axis - referred to as the "independent variable" which are categorical and the Y axis(dependent variable) which is numerical.




The architecture above shows how data is "inputted" to the model and how it is processed. Based on the data, it will make a prediction or result, which is known as an "output" Between this, the model uses Bi-directional LTSM networks. It is capable of learning long-term dependencies in data sequences which allows it to retain certain information for longer. The data is then passed to the time-distribution layer and dense layer to classify each word index that falls into a category of a respected entity or not.



Stage 2: Mapping

The x variable is then mapped with the y. Mapping is dependent on the distances between the corresponding entities. We call it our baseline model for this task
